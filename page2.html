The example above can give you an understanding of how multiple users read or view a website. The way we read text, say maybe a book, is that we always read it left to right. A good example is how comic books can be structured. A majority of how comic books are structured is that the images are displayed and ordered from left to right, same thing when a character is talking in a comic book, the start of the dialogue would be on the far left of the little image box or whatever it is called.

The left side of your website is where the users will look the most.

look at this eye tracking heatmap done by Nielsen Norman Group,

If you look at the heat markings, you will notice that it is in the shape of an "F". This is why it is important for your website to account for this, because if you don't, your user could miss many important things on your website.

You could've also noticed that the advertisements are on the right side of the screen, this shows that the web designer may be aware of the "F" design pattern, so they accounted for it by putting the advertisements on the right side of the screen, because the advertisements are not a priority for the web page.
